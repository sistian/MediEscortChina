City Overview

About Guangzhou

Discover Guangzhou — where world-class medical care meets rich culture, history, and unforgettable experiences.

📊 City Facts

Area
7,434 km²
Population
18.7 million
Name Origin

Guangzhou (广州) means "Wide State". It was founded as Panyu in 214 BC during the Qin Dynasty. It has been a major trading port for over 2,000 years and was the starting point of the Maritime Silk Road. The name Guangzhou was officially adopted during the Three Kingdoms period.

Happiness & Quality of Life

Guangzhou ranks high in happiness due to its mild climate, excellent food, and strong healthcare system. It has the highest life expectancy in China (84.3 years) and the most hospitals per capita in the Pearl River Delta.

🚇 Metro & Education

Public Transportation

Guangzhou Metro has 16 lines and 302 stations. Connected to all major medical centers: Sun Yat-sen University Cancer Center (Line 5), Guangdong Provincial People's Hospital (Line 1), and the First Affiliated Hospital of Guangzhou Medical University (Line 2/8). High-speed rail connects to Hong Kong in 48 minutes.

Universities & Medical Schools

Guangzhou has 82 universities including Sun Yat-sen University (QS #260), South China University of Technology, and Southern Medical University — all with top-tier medical schools.

Natural Resources & Environment

Guangzhou sits on the Pearl River Delta with mild subtropical climate. The city has 1,800+ parks and green spaces, including Baiyun Mountain ("White Cloud Mountain") and the Pearl River waterfront. It's nicknamed the "City of Flowers" for its year-round blooms.

Attractions

Must-See Places in Guangzhou

Explore Guangzhou's iconic landmarks — all selected for accessibility and comfort during medical recovery.

📍

Canton Tower

Guangzhou's iconic TV tower (600m tall). Observation deck with panoramic views. Wheelchair accessible via elevator.

📍

Shamian Island

Colonial-era European architecture on a sandbank in the Pearl River. Flat, pedestrian-only streets lined with banyan trees. Perfect for gentle walks during recovery.

📍

Chen Clan Ancestral Hall

Qing Dynasty academic temple with exquisite Canton ivory carving, ceramics, and embroidery. UNESCO-level craftsmanship.

📍

Baiyun Mountain

"White Cloud Mountain" — the city's green lung. Cable car to the summit. Fresh air and scenic views. Ideal for light exercise between treatments.

📍

Temple of the Five Immortals

Legendary temple where five immortals rode goats to bless Guangzhou with a bountiful harvest. The origin of Guangzhou's nickname "City of Five Rams".

📍

Pearl River Night Cruise

Evening boat cruise along the Pearl River with illuminated skyline views. Minimal walking required — perfect for patients in recovery.

Local Cuisine

Food & Dining in Guangzhou

Savor Guangzhou's legendary cuisine. We've selected dishes suitable for medical recovery — soft, nutritious, and delicious.

🍽️

Dim Sum (Yum Cha)

Guangzhou is the birthplace of dim sum. Shrimp dumplings (har gow), BBQ pork buns (char siu bao), and egg tarts at restaurants like Panxi and Tao Tao Ju. Soft and easy to eat.

🍽️

White Cut Chicken (Baiqie Ji)

Poached chicken served with ginger-scallion sauce. Guangzhou's signature dish. Tender, healthy, and perfect for recovery.

🍽️

Roast Goose (Siu Ngo)

Cantonese-style roast goose with crispy skin and tender meat. Famous at Bing Sheng Restaurant.

🍽️

Wonton Noodles

Silky egg noodles with shrimp wontons in clear broth. A Guangzhou comfort food staple. Soft and easy to digest.

🍽️

Double-Skin Milk (Shuang Pi Nai)

Steamed milk pudding with two layers of skin. A sweet, creamy dessert. Guangzhou's famous dessert at Nanxin.

🍽️

Snake Soup (She Geng)

Traditional winter delicacy with shredded snake meat, mushrooms, and chrysanthemum petals. Believed to improve circulation and skin health.

Shopping

Shopping in Guangzhou

From traditional medicine markets to luxury boutiques — Guangzhou offers something for every shopper.

🛍️

Beijing Road

Guangzhou's main pedestrian shopping street with 1,000+ years of commercial history. Mix of ancient ruins and modern stores.

🛍️

Shangxiajiu Pedestrian Street

Traditional Cantonese shopping district with local brands, herbal medicine shops, and dim sum restaurants.

🛍️

Tee Mall (Tianhe)

Guangzhou's largest luxury shopping mall. International brands, duty-free shops, and international pharmacies.

🛍️

Qingping Medicine Market

China's largest traditional Chinese medicine market. Over 1,000 stalls selling herbs, roots, and medicinal animals. A fascinating visit for medical tourists.

🛍️

Haiyin Square

Electronics and fabric market near the Pearl River. Great for budget shopping and finding local specialties.
