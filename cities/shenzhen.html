City Overview

About Shenzhen

Discover Shenzhen — where world-class medical care meets rich culture, history, and unforgettable experiences.

📊 City Facts

Area
1,997 km²
Population
17.6 million
Name Origin

Shenzhen (深圳) literally means "Deep Drainage" — referring to the deep irrigation channels in the area. Before 1979, it was a small fishing village of 30,000 people. In 1980, it became China's first Special Economic Zone and transformed into a megacity in just 40 years.

Happiness & Quality of Life

Shenzhen is China's youngest major city, known for its innovation, efficiency, and clean environment. It ranks high in livability with excellent air quality, modern infrastructure, and proximity to Hong Kong.

🚇 Metro & Education

Public Transportation

Shenzhen Metro has 16 lines and 369 stations. Connected to major medical centers: Peking University Shenzhen Hospital (Line 2), Shenzhen People's Hospital (Line 3), and the University of Hong Kong-Shenzhen Hospital (Line 2). The airport is connected via Metro Line 11.

Universities & Medical Schools

Shenzhen has 15 universities including Southern University of Science and Technology (SUSTech), Shenzhen University, and the Chinese University of Hong Kong (Shenzhen). The city invests heavily in biomedical research and healthcare innovation.

Natural Resources & Environment

Shenzhen is one of China's greenest cities with 45% forest coverage, 1,200+ parks (the most per capita in China), and a 200km coastline. The city is famous for its tech parks and innovation zones.

Must-See Places in Shenzhen

Explore Shenzhen's iconic landmarks — all selected for accessibility and comfort during medical recovery.

Window of the World

Theme park with 130 replicas of world-famous landmarks. A fun, flat, and wheelchair-friendly way to "see the world" during recovery days.

📍

Shenzhen Bay Park

15km waterfront promenade with stunning views of Hong Kong across the bay. Perfect for gentle rehabilitation walks and sunset viewing.

📍

Dafen Oil Painting Village

Artist village where thousands of painters produce replicas and original artworks. A relaxing cultural experience with cafes and galleries.

📍

OCT Loft Creative Culture Park

Converted factory complex turned art district with galleries, design studios, and cafes. Wheelchair accessible and indoors — ideal for light cultural activity.

📍

Lianhua Mountain Park

Central city park with a large statue of Deng Xiaoping. Flat walking paths and a hilltop viewpoint accessible by road.

📍

Shenzhen Museum

Free museum with exhibits on the city's transformation from fishing village to megacity. Wheelchair accessible and air-conditioned.

Fresh seafood from the South China Sea. Steamed fish, salt-and-pepper squid, and garlic prawns are local favorites. Light and nutritious for recovery.

Chaoshan Beef Hot Pot

Tender, thinly sliced beef cooked in a clear broth. Famous chains like Baheli serve premium beef. Nutritious and easy to digest.

Shenzhen Dim Sum

Cantonese-style dim sum with a modern twist. Steamed dumplings, BBQ pork buns, and egg tarts are available citywide.

Stuffed Tofu (Yong Doufu)

Hakka-style tofu stuffed with minced pork and vegetables. Soft, protein-rich, and easy to eat. Popular in Shenzhen's Hakka restaurants.

Grilled skewers of meat, seafood, and vegetables at street-side stalls. A lively evening experience for adventurous eaters.

Coconut Chicken Hot Pot
