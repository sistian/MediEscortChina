City Overview

About Chengdu

Discover Chengdu — where world-class medical care meets rich culture, history, and unforgettable experiences.

📊 City Facts

Area
14,335 km²
Population
21.2 million
Name Origin

Chengdu (成都) means "Becoming a Capital". The name dates back over 2,300 years to the Warring States period when King Hui of Shu established it as his capital. It has been one of China's most important cultural and economic centers ever since.

Happiness & Quality of Life

Chengdu consistently ranks as China's happiest city. Known for its laid-back lifestyle, teahouse culture, and abundant green spaces. Healthcare quality is high with the West China Hospital (Sichuan University) ranked #2 in China.

🚇 Metro & Education

Public Transportation

Chengdu Metro has 12 lines and 373 stations covering 518 km. All major hospitals are connected: West China Hospital (Line 1/3), Sichuan Provincial People's Hospital (Line 2), and Chengdu Military General Hospital (Line 3). Metro connects directly to Tianfu International Airport.

Universities & Medical Schools

Chengdu has 65 universities including Sichuan University (QS #336) and its world-renowned West China Medical Center, Southwest Jiaotong University, and University of Electronic Science and Technology.

Natural Resources & Environment

Chengdu sits in the fertile Chengdu Plain surrounded by the Longmen Mountains. The Dujiangyan Irrigation System (2,256 years old) still waters the plain. The city has 1,500+ parks and is known as the "City of Parks".

Attractions

Must-See Places in Chengdu

Explore Chengdu's iconic landmarks — all selected for accessibility and comfort during medical recovery.

📍

Giant Panda Breeding Base

World-famous panda research and breeding center 30 minutes from downtown. Flat, wheelchair-friendly paths. A must-visit for patients of all ages — pandas are therapeutic!

📍

Jinli Ancient Street

Qing Dynasty-style pedestrian street with traditional teahouses, Sichuan opera performances, and snack vendors. Gentle walking, plenty of benches for rest.

📍

Wuhou Shrine

Memorial temple to Zhuge Liang, the legendary strategist of the Three Kingdoms. Peaceful gardens with ancient cypress trees.

📍

Dujiangyan Irrigation System

2,256-year-old engineering marvel still in use. UNESCO World Heritage. Flat walking paths along the Min River — ideal for light exercise during recovery.

📍

Qingcheng Mountain

Taoist sacred mountain with ancient temples. The front mountain has cable car access. Lush forests with excellent air quality — beneficial for respiratory recovery.

📍

Kuanzhai Alley

Restored Qing Dynasty alleyways with courtyard restaurants, teahouses, and traditional medicine shops. Flat and accessible.

Local Cuisine

Food & Dining in Chengdu

Savor Chengdu's legendary cuisine. We've selected dishes suitable for medical recovery — soft, nutritious, and delicious.

🍽️

Sichuan Hot Pot

Chengdu's soul food — bubbling chili oil broth with meats, vegetables, and tofu. Famous brands: Haidilao (excellent service, English menu), Xiaolongfan, and Shu Jiuxiang.

🍽️

Mapo Tofu

Spicy, numbing tofu with minced pork. Soft, protein-rich, and easy to eat. Originated in Chengdu.

🍽️

Kung Pao Chicken

World-famous Sichuan dish — diced chicken, peanuts, and chili peppers. Sweet, sour, and spicy. Tender and easy to chew.

🍽️

Dan Dan Noodles

Street food classic — spicy noodles with minced pork, preserved vegetables, and chili oil. A Chengdu breakfast staple.

🍽️

Chuan Chuan Xiang (Skewered Snacks)

Skewers of meat and vegetables boiled in spicy broth. Eat at a street-side stall for the authentic experience.

🍽️

Rabbit Heads

Chengdu's famous snack — spicy marinated rabbit heads. An acquired taste, but a local legend. Soft meat, easy to eat.

Shopping in Chengdu

From traditional medicine markets to luxury boutiques — Chengdu offers something for every shopper.

🛍️

Chunxi Road

Chengdu's premier shopping district with international brands, local boutiques, and traditional medicine shops. Connected to Metro Line 2.

🛍️

Songxianqiao Art Market

China's second-largest antique market after Beijing's Panjiayuan. Traditional medicine tools, calligraphy, and Tibetan crafts.

🛍️

Tianfu Square Underground Mall

Massive underground shopping complex beneath Chengdu's central square. Clothing, electronics, and health products.

🛍️

Wuhou Shrine Street

Traditional Chinese medicine shops, Shu embroidery, and Sichuan tea. Buy Tibetan medicinal herbs from nearby regions.

🛍️

Chengdu International Finance Square (IFS)

Luxury shopping mall with a giant climbing panda statue. High-end brands and international pharmacies.
