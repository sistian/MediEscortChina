City Overview

About Beijing

Discover Beijing — where world-class medical care meets rich culture, history, and unforgettable experiences.

📊 City Facts

Area
16,410 km²
Population
21.8 million
Name Origin

Beijing (北京) literally means "Northern Capital". It was first established as a capital during the Jin Dynasty (1153 AD) and has served as the capital for Yuan, Ming, and Qing dynasties. The name was officially adopted in 1403 during the Ming Dynasty when the Yongle Emperor moved the capital from Nanjing.

Happiness & Quality of Life

Beijing consistently ranks in the top 10 of China's city happiness index, with strong scores in healthcare access (4 hospitals per 1,000 residents), education resources, and cultural amenities.

🚇 Metro & Education

Public Transportation

Beijing's subway is the world's longest metro network with 27 lines and 490+ stations spanning 836 km. Trains run every 2-3 minutes during peak hours. Major medical hubs are connected: Peking Union (Line 1/5), 301 Hospital (Line 1), and Tiantan Hospital (Line 5/8).

Universities & Medical Schools

Home to 92 universities including Peking University (QS #12), Tsinghua University (QS #20), and Beijing Union Medical College — one of China's premier medical schools.

Natural Resources & Environment

Beijing has 21 municipal parks and 11 national parks within 100km, including the Fragrant Hills (Xiangshan) and Yanqing's Songshan National Nature Reserve. The city has 5 major rivers and 26 reservoirs.

Attractions

Must-See Places in Beijing

Explore Beijing's iconic landmarks — all selected for accessibility and comfort during medical recovery.

📍

Forbidden City

The world's largest palace complex, 980 buildings across 72 hectares. UNESCO World Heritage. Ideal for visitors recovering from medical procedures to experience imperial culture in a flat, wheelchair-friendly environment.

📍

Temple of Heaven

Ming Dynasty temple complex where emperors prayed for harvest. A peaceful park perfect for morning tai chi and gentle walks post-treatment.

📍

Summer Palace

Qing Dynasty imperial garden with Kunming Lake and Longevity Hill. Beautiful for rehabilitation walks along the lake promenade.

📍

Great Wall (Mutianyu)

Less crowded section of the Great Wall with cable car access. Not recommended immediately after surgery, but excellent for patients in recovery weeks.

📍

798 Art District

Former factory complex turned contemporary art hub. Wheelchair accessible and indoors — ideal for light cultural activity during recovery.

📍

Hutong Walking Tour

Traditional Beijing alleyways around Nanluoguxiang. Gentle walks with tea houses for rest stops.

Local Cuisine

Food & Dining in Beijing

Savor Beijing's legendary cuisine. We've selected dishes suitable for medical recovery — soft, nutritious, and delicious.

🍽️

Peking Duck

The iconic dish — crispy skin, tender meat, served with pancakes. Quanjude and Da Dong are legendary. A soft, nutritious option even for dental patients (shredded meat without skin).

🍽️

Zhajiangmian (Noodles with Soybean Paste)

Beijing's signature comfort food. Soft noodles with savory meat sauce — ideal for post-dental or post-surgery recovery.

🍽️

Mongolian Hot Pot

Donglaishun serves Beijing's most famous mutton hot pot. Nutritious broth, tender meat — great for building strength during medical recovery.

🍽️

Jiaozi (Dumplings)

Every Beijinger's comfort food. Steamed dumplings with pork and cabbage are easy to chew and digest.

🍽️

Ludagunr (Glutinous Rice Rolls)

Traditional Beijing snack — sweet red bean filling wrapped in sticky rice and soybean flour. Soft and energy-rich.

🍽️

Wangfujing Snack Street

For adventurous eaters: scorpion skewers, candied hawthorn, and street-side lamb kebabs.

Shopping

Shopping in Beijing

From traditional medicine markets to luxury boutiques — Beijing offers something for every shopper.

🛍️

Wangfujing Street

Beijing's premier shopping street with international brands and traditional Chinese medicine pharmacies. Many medical tourists purchase herbal supplements here.

🛍️

Silk Street (Xiushui Market)

Famous for silk products, pearls, and custom tailoring. Great for buying gifts while recovering.

🛍️

Panjiayuan Antique Market

Weekend market for antiques, calligraphy, and traditional Chinese medicine artifacts. A cultural experience for visitors with time between appointments.

🛍️

Dashilan (Dazhalan)

Historic commercial street near Tiananmen. Home to Tongrentang (founded 1669), China's most prestigious traditional medicine pharmacy.
