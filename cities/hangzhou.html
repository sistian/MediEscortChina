City Overview

About Hangzhou

Discover Hangzhou — where world-class medical care meets rich culture, history, and unforgettable experiences.

📊 City Facts

Area
16,596 km²
Population
12.0 million
Name Origin

Hangzhou (杭州) means "Hangzhou Prefecture". The name was first used in the Sui Dynasty (589 AD). It was the capital of the Southern Song Dynasty (1127-1279) and described by Marco Polo as "the finest and most splendid city in the world."

Happiness & Quality of Life

Hangzhou consistently ranks as one of China's most livable cities. Known for its work-life balance, beautiful environment, and high-tech economy. It's home to Alibaba and has a thriving digital health ecosystem.

🚇 Metro & Education

Public Transportation

Hangzhou Metro has 12 lines and 254 stations. Connected to major medical centers: First Affiliated Hospital of Zhejiang University (Line 1/5), Sir Run Run Shaw Hospital (Line 2), and Hangzhou Red Cross Hospital (Line 1/5). The airport is connected via Metro Line 1 and 7.

Universities & Medical Schools

Hangzhou has 47 universities including Zhejiang University (QS #44), one of China's top 3 universities, and China Academy of Art. Zhejiang University School of Medicine is ranked among the top 5 in China.

Natural Resources & Environment

Hangzhou is famous for West Lake (Xihu), a UNESCO World Heritage site. The city has over 3,000 km² of water surface including the Grand Canal, Qiantang River, and Xixi Wetland. It has 1,700+ parks and is called the "City of Heaven on Earth".

Attractions

Must-See Places in Hangzhou

Explore Hangzhou's iconic landmarks — all selected for accessibility and comfort during medical recovery.

📍

West Lake (Xihu)

UNESCO World Heritage site — the most beautiful lake in China. Flat walking paths around the 15km shoreline. Perfect for gentle exercise during recovery. Su Causeway and Bai Causeway are must-walks.

📍

Lingyin Temple

1,600-year-old Buddhist temple nestled in the Feilai Feng hills. Accessible by shuttle bus. Peaceful gardens and ancient grottoes.

📍

Xixi National Wetland Park

China's first national wetland park. Boat tours through waterways and reed beds. Minimal walking required — ideal for patients in recovery.

📍

Longjing Tea Village

Home to Dragon Well (Longjing) tea, China's most famous green tea. Visit tea plantations, learn about tea culture, and taste fresh spring tea.

📍

Grand Canal

UNESCO World Heritage — the world's longest artificial river. Historic section in Hangzhou with stone bridges and waterside teahouses.

📍

Leifeng Pagoda

Iconic pagoda overlooking West Lake. Elevator access to the top for panoramic views. Sunset viewing is legendary.

Local Cuisine

Food & Dining in Hangzhou

Savor Hangzhou's legendary cuisine. We've selected dishes suitable for medical recovery — soft, nutritious, and delicious.

🍽️

West Lake Fish in Vinegar (Song Shu Yu)

Hangzhou's signature dish — freshwater carp with sweet-and-sour sauce. The fish is tender and the sauce is mild. A perfect recovery meal.

🍽️

Dongpo Pork (Dongpo Rou)

Braised pork belly named after Song Dynasty poet Su Dongpo. Melts in the mouth. Extremely soft and easy to eat.

🍽️

Longjing Shrimp

Fresh river shrimp stir-fried with Longjing tea leaves. Light, fragrant, and healthy. A Hangzhou specialty.

🍽️

Beggar's Chicken (Jiao Hua Ji)

Whole chicken wrapped in lotus leaves and clay, then slow-baked. The meat is incredibly tender and falls off the bone.

🍽️

Sister Song's Fish Broth (Song Sao Yu Geng)

A thick fish soup with vinegar and pepper. Hangzhou's most famous breakfast soup. Warm and comforting.

🍽️

Hangzhou Xiaolongbao

Delicate soup dumplings with crab roe and pork filling. Steamed fresh. Soft and flavorful.
